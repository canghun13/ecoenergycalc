Smart Thermostat vs Programmable Thermostat

8 min read · June 2026

Both smart and programmable thermostats can cut your heating and cooling bills by 10–15% annually — but only if used correctly. The difference is that smart thermostats do the work for you, while programmable thermostats require consistent manual setup that many homeowners never actually program.

Programmable Thermostat: Effective If You Use It

A programmable thermostat costs $25–80 and allows you to set different temperatures for different times of day and days of the week. The DOE estimates 10% annual savings if used to set back the temperature 7–10°F for 8 hours per day. The catch: studies have shown that most programmable thermostat owners don't keep their schedules programmed — reducing actual savings to minimal levels. If you have a consistent schedule and will actually program it, a $30 programmable thermostat is excellent value.

Smart Thermostat: Automates the Savings

Smart thermostats (Nest, Ecobee, Honeywell Home) cost $130–250 and learn your schedule, adjust automatically when you leave, and can be controlled remotely. Ecobee claims average savings of 26% on heating and cooling. The DOE-backed estimate for schedule-based setbacks is ~10%. Real-world savings typically fall in the 10–15% range — but this happens automatically without any behavior change required. Many utilities offer $25–75 rebates that reduce the effective cost.

Payback Period

At 10% savings on a $1,500 annual HVAC bill ($150/year saved): programmable thermostat ($50) pays back in 4 months. Smart thermostat ($180 after utility rebate) pays back in 14 months. Both are excellent ROI — the question is whether you'll actually use the programmable thermostat's features.

Which Should You Choose?

  • Programmable: If you have a very consistent schedule and will set it once and leave it. Great for rental properties or budget-constrained situations.
  • Smart thermostat: If you have an irregular schedule, frequently forget to adjust the thermostat, or want remote control and utility integration features. The automation is what most people actually need.
