How Much Can Solar Save You?

The average U.S. homeowner with a 6kW solar system saves $1,200–$1,500 per year on electricity. In high-sun states like Arizona or California, savings can exceed $2,000 annually.

Key Factors That Affect Solar Savings

  • Peak sun hours: Arizona gets ~6.5 hrs/day; Seattle gets ~3.5 hrs/day
  • Electricity rate: Higher rates mean bigger savings from solar
  • Net metering: If your utility credits excess solar, your savings increase
  • Roof orientation: South-facing roofs at 30° tilt are ideal
  • System size: Sized to offset 80–100% of your usage is typical

Federal Solar Tax Credit

The Residential Clean Energy Credit (IRA) provides a 30% tax credit on solar installation costs through 2032. On a $20,000 system, that's $6,000 back from the federal government.
