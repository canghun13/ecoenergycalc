Why This Is a Range, Not an Exact Quote

Unlike a straightforward kWh-times-rate calculation, demand response payouts aren't standardized across utilities the way electricity rates are. Each utility designs its own program: some pay a flat annual bill credit regardless of how many events occur, others scale payment with the number and severity of events in a given summer, and a handful use market-based pricing tied to real-time grid conditions. This estimator uses the published low-to-high range for each program category based on real 2026 programs, but your specific utility's terms are the only fully accurate source for what you'd actually earn.

The Four Program Tiers, Explained

  • Basic AC-cycling / Direct Load Control: A utility technician installs a small switch on your outdoor AC condenser, at no cost to you. During an event, your compressor cycles off for roughly 15-20 minutes per hour. No smart thermostat required — works with any central AC system.
  • Smart Thermostat BYOT (Bring Your Own Thermostat): Works through your existing Wi-Fi-connected thermostat's app rather than a physical switch. Because the utility can adjust your setpoint by small increments instead of cutting power entirely, these programs often pay a modest bonus over basic AC-cycling. See our smart thermostat comparison if you don't have one yet.
  • Premium grid-constrained programs: Utilities serving areas where the local grid is under particular strain (like parts of Southern California) sometimes offer higher payouts to attract enough enrolled capacity in those specific zones.
  • Battery storage or EV charger enrollment: Pays considerably more since a battery or EV charger gives the utility precisely controllable capacity, but requires already owning that hardware — see our battery storage calculator for the economics of the hardware itself.

How to Find Your Actual Program Terms

Search "[your utility name] demand response" or check the "ways to save" section of your utility's website. Coverage varies significantly by region — not every utility offers every tier described here, and some rural cooperatives or municipal utilities may have no residential demand response program at all. Frequently Asked Questions

Is this demand response calculator exact for my utility?

No — treat it as an order-of-magnitude estimate, not a quote. Residential demand response payouts vary significantly by utility, region, and specific program design. This estimator uses published ranges from major U.S. residential programs (basic AC-cycling, smart thermostat BYOT, and battery/EV enrollment) to give you a realistic ballpark before you check your own utility's specific program terms.

Why do most demand response calculators online seem built for businesses, not homes?

Most publicly available demand response calculators (from providers like Enel X, Sympower, and similar aggregators) are built for commercial and industrial facilities curtailing large loads measured in kilowatts or megawatts, since that's where the biggest, most standardized revenue programs are. Residential programs pay far less per household and are structured very differently (flat seasonal bill credits rather than per-kW market payments), which is why a dedicated homeowner-facing estimator is harder to find.

Does participating in demand response affect my thermostat settings permanently?

No. Demand response only adjusts your thermostat or cycles your AC during specific, limited "event" days — typically a handful of the hottest afternoons each summer, capped at a set number of total hours per season. Outside of those events, your thermostat and AC operate exactly as you've set them, with no permanent change to your settings or comfort.
