About EcoEnergyCalc

About This Site

EcoEnergyCalc is an independent website run by an individual with a background in energy and sustainability topics. The site is not affiliated with any government agency, utility company, or energy industry organization. All tools and articles are produced independently.

What We Do

EcoEnergyCalc is a free collection of energy cost and carbon footprint calculators. Whether you're trying to figure out if solar panels are worth it, understand your electric bill, or calculate your carbon footprint, we've built a tool for it.

All tools are free, require no signup, and deliver instant results. No paywalls. No hidden fees. Just answers.

Why We Built This

Understanding your energy use and environmental impact shouldn't require a spreadsheet or an engineering background. Most of the information is out there, but it's scattered across government websites, utility company PDFs, and academic papers that weren't written for regular people.

We built EcoEnergyCalc to put that information in one place — in a format that's easy to use and understand.

How Our Calculators Work

Every calculator on this site is built on established formulas and publicly available data — including EPA emissions factors, EIA energy rates, DOE efficiency guidelines, and peer-reviewed research. Where local data matters (like utility rates or solar irradiance), we use the best available national averages with notes on how to adjust for your area.

Results are estimates based on established formulas and publicly available data. They are intended for general informational purposes only and should not be relied upon as professional financial, tax, engineering, or legal advice. For major decisions — including solar installation, HVAC replacement, or tax credit eligibility — we always recommend consulting qualified professionals.

Energy rates, incentive programs, and product specifications change frequently. While we work to keep information current, we cannot guarantee that all figures reflect the most recent data. Always verify incentive eligibility and product details with the relevant government agency or installer before making purchasing decisions.

Data Sources

We source figures directly from government and research organizations rather than estimating from memory. The main sources behind our calculators are:

  • U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) — average residential electricity rates by state, natural gas prices, and household energy consumption data.
  • EPA Greenhouse Gas Equivalencies Calculator — CO2 emissions factors used in our carbon footprint, tree-offset, and solar CO2 calculators.
  • U.S. Department of Energy (DOE) Energy Saver — appliance efficiency guidelines, insulation R-value recommendations, and heating/cooling best practices.
  • NREL PVWatts / National Solar Radiation Database — peak sun hours and solar production estimates by U.S. region, used in our solar savings and panel-count calculators.
  • Poore & Nemecek (2018), Science — per-food-category carbon footprint figures used in our diet carbon and vegan-vs-meat calculators, one of the most widely cited food-emissions studies.

When a figure comes from an industry average rather than a government dataset (for example, typical appliance wattages or installation costs), we note that in the calculator's result notes so you know exactly what assumption is being used.

How Often We Update

Electricity rates, federal tax credit percentages, and state incentive programs are reviewed and updated as changes are confirmed — most notably the 30% federal solar Investment Tax Credit (ITC) under the Inflation Reduction Act, which is currently scheduled through 2032. If you spot a figure that looks outdated, please let us know and we'll verify it.

How We Keep This Free

EcoEnergyCalc is supported by advertising rather than subscriptions or paywalls. We don't sell your data, require an account, or gate any calculator behind a signup form. Ads are served through Google AdSense; we don't hand-pick advertisers or accept sponsored placements that would bias a calculator's results.
